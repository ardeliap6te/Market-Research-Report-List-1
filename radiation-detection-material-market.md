<p><p>The radiation detection material market comprises three primary types: semiconductors, scintillators, and others. Semiconductors, such as silicon or germanium, effectively convert radiation into electrical signals, offering high resolution and sensitivity. Scintillators are materials that emit light when exposed to radiation, making them suitable for detecting and measuring various radiation types. The "others" category includes specialized materials like gas-filled detectors and fiber-optic systems, which serve niche applications in radiation detection across sectors like medical, industrial, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
