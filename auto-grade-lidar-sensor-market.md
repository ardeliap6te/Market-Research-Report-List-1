<p><p>The auto-grade LiDAR sensor market is primarily divided into two types: Mechanical LiDAR and Solid-State LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR employs rotating components to capture 360-degree views, making it effective for detailed mapping but potentially less durable due to moving parts. In contrast, Solid-State LiDAR uses electronic sensors with no moving parts, offering enhanced reliability and compact designs, ideal for automotive applications. Both types are crucial for advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and autonomous vehicle technology development.</p></p>
