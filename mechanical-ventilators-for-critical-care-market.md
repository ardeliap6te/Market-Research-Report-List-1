<p><strong>The Mechanical Ventilators for Critical Care Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Invasive</li><li>Non-Invasive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Mechanical ventilators in critical care are categorized into invasive and non-invasive types. Invasive ventilators require an artificial airway, like an endotracheal tube, allowing precise control of airflow and pressure for patients with severe respiratory issues. Non-invasive ventilators, on the other hand, use masks or nasal prongs, enabling support without intubation, making them suitable for conditions like COPD or sleep apnea. Both types are essential for managing respiratory failure, ensuring oxygenation, and maintaining patient comfort and safety in critical settings.</p></p>
